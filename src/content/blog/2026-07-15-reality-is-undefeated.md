Unless you are a big fish in a big pond, we will be making fun of you every day here on out.

Let me explain. My son got screwed over last year during the college admission process. Yeah. I guess he doesn't have 1000 extracurriculars, or he probably has a shitty personality. At least that's what the super duper smart college admission officers decided.

Here is a huge problem with the current United States. They fucked up the system so much that what used to be signals are no longer signals. Elite schools used to mean you were smart if you went there. Jobs too. I think it's still true in blue collar professions. But does anyone actually believe that people who have jobs are any smarter or better at what they do?

When I hear so-and-so went to Harvard or something these days, the first thing that comes to my mind is that he must have played the game the right way and probably had an expensive private college counselor to boot.

Why do I assume that? My son's friends. About 99% of them ended up in top 10-12ish colleges, including MIT, Stanford, etc. It hasn't even been a year, but I hate to report that they aren't doing too well. It turns out they weren't meant to be there, and they are playing NPCs in their own schools.

It sucks because those seats could have gone to kids who actually deserved to be there. For them, the diploma could have boosted them to something higher. But now these undeserving kids have the diploma, and they won't accomplish anything with it.

I just saw a kid at Stanford selling some crappy prompted AI tool on Nextdoor. He can "automate new employee onboarding and cut it down to 80%!" Wow. You went to Stanford to build a shitty AI wrapper that nobody would pay money for?

See? The thing about life is that reality is undefeated.

I'm kind of shocked at how quickly reality is recalibrating and rebalancing. It hasn't even been a year, and these kids are not doing too good.

Sorry, not sorry.
