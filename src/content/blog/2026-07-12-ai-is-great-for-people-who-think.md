AI is an awesome product for people who think.

If you are the type of person who walks in the morning around the neighborhood and ponders on things, AI is great. If you are a person who sits on a couch all day and doomscrolls, it won't help you much.

You have to have good ideas in your head. You have to fill your brain with ideas. Ideas come from knowing things. Better ideas come from knowing multiple domains.

Here is one of many hypotheses why biology isn't advancing as fast as we hoped: there aren't that many truly smart people who have multi-domain expertise. Many times, new ideas come from other domains.

So here we are.

Playing with AI every day shows me what I lack: I don't think fast enough. I don't think hard enough. I don't have enough ideas to fully utilize AI. I am not smart enough to gather what AI gives me and make sense out of it.

So far, AI has made "gathering information" and "learning about anything" super fast and cheap, but it's still up to humans to process all that and make something new out of it.

And I am skeptical that AI will ever reach that point.

I will be glad to be wrong on that.
